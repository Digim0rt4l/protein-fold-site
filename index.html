What’s actually happening here

This page is running a simplified, coarse-grained (Cα-only) conformational search on amyloid-beta (1–42), the peptide (PDB entry 1IYT) whose aggregation is central to the amyloid hypothesis of Alzheimer’s disease.

Every visitor's device is handed a small "work unit" — a short stretch of the backbone — and runs a short simulated-annealing search on it in a Web Worker. Results are sent to a Netlify Function, independently re-scored on the server, and merged into one shared structure (stored as JSON in this project's GitHub repo) only when they genuinely lower the total energy.

Please calibrate your expectations: this is an educational / citizen-science-style demo of distributed computing and coarse-grained energy minimization — built for learning and engagement, not a production molecular-dynamics engine like Folding@home, and not a substitute for tools like AlphaFold or Rosetta. It won't produce new, publishable Alzheimer's science on its own, but it's a genuine, functioning distributed system you can watch work in real time.
